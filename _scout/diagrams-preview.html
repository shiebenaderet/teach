Scouting preview · Diagram system

Two prototype diagrams for the hub

These are early sketches of a visual grammar for the Discussion Hub's protocols — inspired by Oliver Caviglioli's 2-color pedagogical diagrams (ink + one accent, generous whitespace, gold row-labels, clean sans-serif type). The goal: give each WalkThru a spatial picture teachers can glance at before reading the steps.

Two complementary examples below. Fishbowl is a spatial diagram — it shows who sits where. Structured Academic Controversy is a procedural diagram — it shows how the conversation moves through five phases. Between them they cover most of what the other WalkThrus will need.

Notes on the system

Both diagrams use the same small vocabulary: ink outline for "the default / listening role," maroon fill for "the active / arguing role," gold for teacher / transition moments, cream for background, dashed lines for silent relationships (pairings, transitions, observation).

Once this vocabulary is locked, every other WalkThru gets its diagram almost for free — jigsaw is SAC's band structure with three rows instead of five; Socratic Seminar is Fishbowl's concentric layout with a text node at the center; Think-Pair-Share is a single band with two dots that duplicate then merge.
